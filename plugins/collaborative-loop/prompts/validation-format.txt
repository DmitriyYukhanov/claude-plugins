Structure your validation output EXACTLY in this format:

## Validation of Driver Analysis

### Confirmed Findings

For each of the driver's findings you agree with:
- [original-N] CONFIRMED — <optional refinement or additional context>
  - Severity: <agree | adjusted: original → new, because...>

### Rejected Findings

For each of the driver's findings you believe are false positives or incorrect:
- [original-N] REJECTED — <specific, concrete reason with evidence>

### New Findings

Issues the driver missed entirely:
- [severity: critical|high|medium|minor] [category] File:line — description
  - Suggested fix: <concrete fix>

Severity guide:
- critical: blocks progress, security vulnerability, data loss risk
- high: causes bugs, architectural problems, incorrect behavior
- medium: quality issues, inconsistency, maintainability concerns
- minor: style nits, nice-to-have improvements

Categories: security, performance, correctness, testing, maintainability, completeness, consistency, feasibility

### Summary

CONFIRMED: X of Y original findings
REJECTED: Z of Y original findings
NEW: W additional findings

STATUS: <VALIDATED | PARTIALLY_VALIDATED | REJECTED>
- VALIDATED: all or nearly all findings confirmed, no critical rejections
- PARTIALLY_VALIDATED: some confirmed, some rejected — proceed with confirmed + new only
- REJECTED: majority of findings rejected — driver's analysis needs rework

Rules:
- You MUST address EVERY finding from the driver — do not skip any
- CONFIRMED means you independently verified the issue exists
- REJECTED means you checked and the issue does not exist or is not a real problem
- Provide concrete evidence for rejections (e.g., "line 42 handles this case because...")
- New findings follow the same severity guide as the original review
- Be thorough but honest — false confirmations are as harmful as false rejections
